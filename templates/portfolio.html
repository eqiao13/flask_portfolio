Harmony In Ramen

  • Date: Spring 2016
  • Client: Paperboard Packaging Alliance Student Competition
  • Category: Packaging Design
  • Project members:
    Eve Qiao
    Research, Logo Design, Packaging Design, Print Design
    Aline Brantis
    Research, Logo Design, Packaging Design, Print Design
    Marc Entenberg
    Research, Package Structural Design
    Maegan Schmauder
    Research, Package Structural Design

In this project, we were given the task to heighten the value of a currently low value dry food product that is on the market. We chose cup ramen because when people mention them, they usually think that it is cheap and unhealthy. Because of this, we thought that this product would have the most potential. We want to change that perception for consumers, so that they realize that there is potential for a healthier meal in the cup ramen market. Our concept for a new bowl is to have a bigger portion size with a variety of healthier ingredients so that customers can have a healthy and filling meal.

Our package is portable, but also easy to store at home. The package includes chopsticks, which makes bringing and making lunch simpler and easier. Our bowl-shaped package opens up to a bigger size, so there is more room after the noodles have expanded from cooking. After cooking, the three flaps that keeps the package closed can be easily teared off for eating.

Stress Relief Companion

  • Date: Fall 2015
  • Client: RIT Wellness
  • Category: App InVision Prototype Design
  • Project members:
    Eve Qiao
    Wireframe, Site Map, Research, Print Design, User research and testing, Website creation
    Mary Stalter
    Wireframe, Copywriting, Research, Print Design, User research and testing, Website creation
    Liah Perez
    Wireframe, Website creation, Research, User research and testing
    Nisha Sposato
    Design comps, Brand development, User testing, Print Design

College students are bombarded by many different types of stressors. This collaborative project aims to give college students at RIT some insights to why they are stressed and ways to reduce their stress in a healthy manner. Insquire is a self-guided app that will inquire users about their feelings, and why they are stressed. [Please click here to view our process work.]

Insquire will then help them find the most efficient ways to relieve their stress depending on their particular situation.

Our app will collect the data of the users’ feelings, activities, and apps they have tried, to give insight about how they are feeling on a day to day basis in an interactive graph.

Insquire is unique in that it can be paired up with a smart phone’s default health app or a fitness tracker, like Fitbit, to gather data and ask the users how they are feeling based on that data. For example, if the user wasn’t getting a lot of sleep lately, Insquire will notify the user and ask how they are doing. This makes the app a companion that will be helpful even if the user is not actively using the app.

Living Lolita

  • Date: Fall 2015
  • Client: Kirakira: Global Lolita Meetup
  • Category: Branding and System Design

Kirakira is an international annual Lolita meetup event that aims to connect Lolitas around the world. Lolita is a fashion that originated from Japan in the late 1970s, which is also used to refer to the people who wear the style. It is a cute, elegant, and modest style that went against the trend at the time that revealed more skin. There are many substyles under Lolita, each having its own unique feel.

The identity for Kirakira reflects the Lolita clothing and lifestyle. The identity should be flexible, cute, and elegant to match the Lolita fashion. The accompanying systems design pieces each reflected upon the brand with variations.

Website

The website is mainly focused on information and registration for the event. There are also pages featuring a brand each month and a gallery for Lolitas to share their coordinates.

App

The Kirakira app is a place where Lolitas can socialized. The main feature is the meetup and Lolita locator. The meetup let’s Lolitas set up and attend meetup near then with ease. The locator help Lolita find each other to build a community.

Collateral

These pieces help advertise the event with emphasis on individual substyles and community.

The bus wrap shows of the cuteness of the style.

Attendees can choose the wristband color that matches their coordinate with bow pattern on the inside.

Answering Tough Questions

  • Date: Spring 2016
  • Client: Now You Know
  • Category: Responsive Web Design

Lots of parents are very awkward when it comes to answering their child’s “how do I know if I’m gay?” and other complicated questions. They are not prepared and can be startled or embarrassed to answer these questions that are being thrown at them.

It is important that parents are prepared to give age-appropriate answers to these kind of questions with confidence and credibility.

Newsletter Run

  • Date: May 2018 to Present
  • Client: Measured Marketing Inc.
  • Category: E-Newsletter Design

MMi Monthly is a newsletter designed for Measured Marketing Inc. to send out to sales representatives to help them increase the client base and help clients decide when to send relevant email adverts and website banner adverts with tips and suggestions. I was tasked to create these monthly newsletters from provided body copies. This assignment was very open and I was able to choose and implement a design style that would be used across all the newsletters. I decided to go with a style that uses simple graphics to help visualize key points and separate each section into easily digestible pieces.
